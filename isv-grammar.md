## Phonology

### Hard vs. Soft Consonants
**Soft consonants**: Š Ž Č C J LJ NJ (and sometimes Ć Đ Ś Ź).  
After soft consonants: **-o → -e**, **-y → -i**, **-ě → -i** in endings.

### Key Morphophonemic Alternations
- **Palatalisation**: K→Č, G→Ž, H→Š before certain suffixes/endings (also: C→Č)
  - e.g. ruka → ručny; Bog → Bože (vocative)
- **Iotation**: when hard consonant precedes J: labials stay (but gain palatalization), K/G/H→Č/Ž/Š, L→LJ, N→NJ, R→RJ
- **Fleeting vowel**: movable -o/-e appears in masculine nominative singular and some genitive plurals before consonant clusters

## Nouns

### Gender: Masculine / Feminine / Neuter
- Masculine: nouns ending in consonant (most), a few ending -a (male persons: sluga, kolega)
- Feminine: nouns in -a, and consonant-final nouns (3rd declension: kost)
- Neuter: nouns in -o, -e

### Animacy
**Critical rule**: animate nouns (animals and male persons) have Accusative = Genitive.  
Inanimate nouns have Accusative = Nominative.  
This also affects **adjective and past-tense participle agreement in the plural**:
- Masculine animate plural (nominative): adjective ends **-i**, participle ends **-li**
- Masculine inanimate plural (nominative/accusative): adjective ends **-e**, participle ends **-le**

### Seven Cases
Nominative, Accusative, Genitive, Dative, Instrumental, Locative, Vocative

### Key Rule: Animacy in the Plural
- Masculine **animate** nominative plural: **-i** (dobri muži)
- Masculine **inanimate** nominative/accusative plural: **-e** (dobre domy)

### Adverb Formation
Replace adjective ending with **-o** (hard) or **-e** (soft):
- dobry → **dobro** (well)
- svěži → **svěže** (freshly)

### Comparative
**Analytic**: *vyše / bolje* + positive form: *vyše prosty* (simpler)

**Synthetic**: replace -y/-i with **-ějši** (hard) / **-ejši** (soft):
- bogaty → bogatějši; slaby → slabějši

Exception: adjectives in **-ky, -eky, -oky** → **-ši** (no -ěj-):
- kratky → **kratši**; vysoky → **vysši**; daleko → **dalje** (adv.)

**Adverbial comparative**: replace -ši with **-e** or **-ěje**:
- novo → **nověje**; čisto → **čistěje**

### Irregular Comparatives
| Positive | Comparative (adj.) | Comparative (adv.) |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| dobry (good) | lěpši / lučši | lěpje / lučše |
| zly (bad) | gorši | gorje |
| veliky (big) | večši / bolši | veče / bolje |
| maly (small) | menši | menje |
| blagy (pleasant) | unši | unje |
| legky (easy) | legši | legše |
| mekky (soft) | mekši | mekše |

### Superlative
Add prefix **naj-** to comparative: *najlěpši*, *najgorši*, *najvysši*  
Or **naj-** to positive: *najnovy*, *najnovo*

### Other Degree Modifiers
- "less / least": *menje / najmenje* + positive
- "too / overly": prefix **prě-** (prěprosty = too simple)
- "not": prefix **ne-** (nedobry = not good)

## Verbs

### Aspect
Every verb is either **imperfective** (ongoing/repeated) or **perfective** (completed).  
Prefixes typically make a verb perfective; secondary imperfectives are derived back:
- **-ati → -yvati/-ivati** (sodržati → sodrživati)
- **-iti → -jati** with vowel lengthening (obnoviti → obnavjati)
- **-nuti → -ati** (oddohnuti → oddyhati)
- monosyllabic + **-vati** (prodati → prodavati)

### Two Main Conjugation Classes

**Class 1** (theme vowel, consonant-ending stems):  
Endings: **-u / -eš / -e / -emo / -ete / -ut**

**Class 2** (-i- stems):  
Endings: **-ju / -iš / -i / -imo / -ite / -et**  
Note: 3pl ends in **-et** (not -ut)

### 1sg Flavorization: -u/-ju vs. -m

The 1sg present has two equally valid variants, used to flavor ISV towards different Slavic traditions:

| Flavor | 1sg ending | Class 1 -ati | Class 1 consonant | Class 2 -iti | musěti |
|--------|-----------|-------------|-------------------|-------------|--------|
| South/East (default) | -u / -ju | dělaju | nesu | hvalju | mušu |
| North/West | -m | dělam | nesem | hvalim | musim |

Both forms are correct ISV. Only 1sg differs; all other persons are the same regardless of flavor.

### Present Tense

| | dělati (1) | nesti (1) | hvaliti (2) |
|--|-----------|----------|------------|
| ja | dělaju / dělam | nesu / nesem | hvalju / hvalim |
| ty | dělaješ | neseš | hvališ |
| on/ona/ono | **dělaje** | **nese** | **hvali** |
| my | dělajemo | nesemo | hvalimo |
| vy | dělajete | nesete | hvalite |
| oni/one | dělajut | nesut | **hvalet** |

> **Important**: ISV 3sg of -ati verbs ends in **-aje** (not Russian *-aet*);  
> 3sg of -ěti verbs ends in **-ěje** (not Russian *-ěet*).  
> Examples: *znati* → 3sg **zna** (or **znaje**); *razuměti* → 3sg **razuměje** (not *razuměet*).

### Past Tense (Compound) — L-Participle + auxiliary *byti*

| | dělati | nesti | hvaliti |
|--|--------|-------|---------|
| ja | jesm dělal(a) | jesm nesl(a) | jesm hvalil(a) |
| ty | jesi dělal(a) | jesi nesl(a) | jesi hvalil(a) |
| on | dělal | nesl | hvalil |
| ona | dělala | nesla | hvalila |
| ono | dělalo | neslo | hvalilo |
| my | jesmo dělali | jesmo nesli | jesmo hvalili |
| vy | jeste dělali | jeste nesli | jeste hvalili |
| oni/one | dělali | nesli | hvalili |

L-participle: infinitive stem + **-l / -la / -lo / -li** (m/f/n/pl)

> **Note on plural**: animate masculine pl. → **-li**; inanimate/feminine pl. → **-ly** or **-li** depending on context, but *-li* is the general standard.  
> Forms **byjo / byja** for past of *byti* are **non-standard** — use **bylo / bila**.

### Simple Past (Archaic/Literary)

Consonant-stem: -eh, -eše, -eše, -ehmo, -este, -ehu

### Imperative
Present stem + **-i** (consonant-final), **-j** (vowel-final), **Ø** (after j):

| | dělati | nesti | hvaliti |
|--|--------|-------|---------|
| 2sg | dělaj | nesi | hvali |
| 1pl | dělajmo | nesimo | hvalimo |
| 2pl | dělajte | nesite | hvalite |

3rd person imperative: particle **nehaj** + 3sg present (*nehaj umre!* = Let him die!)

**Athematic verb imperatives** (irregular — do NOT apply the -i rule):
| Verb | 2sg | 1pl | 2pl |
|------|-----|-----|-----|
| jesti (eat) | **jed** | jedimo | jedite |
| dati (give) | **daj** | dajmo | dajte |
| věděti (know) | **vědi** | věděmo | věděte |

"jesti" 2sg = **jed** (zero ending, root *jed-* from 3pl "jedut"), not *jedi*. Matches Polish *jedz*, Czech *jez*, Russian *ешь*.

### Participles & Verbal Noun

| Type | Suffix | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| Present active | -uči / -eči (after soft) | dělajuči, hvaleči |
| Past active | -vši (vowel stem), -ši (consonant) | sdělavši, ukradši |
| Present passive | -omy / -imy | dělajemy, hvalimy |
| Past passive | -ny / -eny | dělany, hvalen; hvaljen (from -iti) |
| Gerund (verbal noun) | -nje / -tje | dělanje, nesenje, bytje |

Special past passive rules:
- -iti verbs: stem + **-jeny** (hvaliti → hvaljeny)
- -nuti verbs: stem + **-neny** (tegnuti → tegneny)
- monosyllabic stems: **-ty** (bity, čuty)

### Passive Voice
*byti* + past passive participle:  
Present: *ja jesm neseny* | Past: *ja byl neseny* | Future: *ja budu neseny*

### Irregular Verb: *byti* "to be"

| Tense | ja | ty | on/ona/ono | my | vy | oni/one |
|-------|----|----|------------|----|----|---------|
| Present | jesm | jesi | jest (je) | jesmo | jeste | sut |
| Simple past | běh | běše | běše (bě) | běhmo | běste | běhu |
| Compound past | jesm byl(a) | jesi byl(a) | byl/byla/**bylo** | jesmo byli | jeste byli | byli |
| Future | budu | budeš | bude | budemo | budete | budut |
| Conditional | byh | bys | by | byhmo | byste | by |

> **Past tense of byti**: byl (m.sg.) / byla (f.sg.) / **bylo** (n.sg.) / byli (pl.)  
> Do NOT use *byjo* or *byja* — these are South Slavic forms not standard in ISV.

### Irregular Verb: *musěti* "must"
| ja | ty | on/ona/ono | my | vy | oni/one |
|----|----|------------|----|----|---------|
| mušu / musim | musiš | musi | musimo | musite | **muset** |

> 3pl is **muset** (not *musut*).

Conjugates regularly in present; L-participle: **šel / šla / šlo / šli** (also *išel*)

### Verbs of Motion (Three Forms)

| Meaning | Non-directional (habitual) | Directional (impf.) | Perfective |
|---------|--------------------------|---------------------|-----------|
| walk | hoditi | idti | pojdti |
| drive | jezditi | jehati | pojehati |

## Syntax

### Word Order
Default: **Subject – Verb – Object (SVO)**, flexible.  
Modifiers typically precede the noun.

### Questions
- **Yes/no**: intonation alone, or prefix **či**, or suffix particle **li** after focus word
- **Content questions**: interrogative word first, then SVO order

### Negative Sentences
Negation with **ne** before verb. With negative pronouns (nikto, ničto, nikde…) the verb is also negated: *Nikto ne pridet* (Nobody will come).

### Purpose / Final Clauses
Use **že by** or **da by** + conditional:  
*Ona prinosi jabloka, da by děti byli zdrave.*

### Passive Voice
Formed with *byti* + past passive participle. Preferred alternatives:
- Reflexive construction: *Pica dělaje se* (Pizza is being made)
- 3rd person plural without subject: *Govori, že…* (It is said that…)

### Copula *jest*
ISV does **not** drop *jest* the way Russian drops "есть".  
*To jest dobro* ✓ (not *To dobro*)
